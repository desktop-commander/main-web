### 2. Pitch Deck — Needs Simplification

The team went through every slide. The recurring feedback:

- **Too much text, too many explanations.** Dmitry says investors spend 30 seconds per slide — bold headlines matter, not paragraphs. Eduard agrees the text version is hard to parse and that the designer needs to bring it to life.
- **Multiple ways of describing DC is confusing.** We say "Canva for automations" in one place, "technical assistant" in another, "last mile" elsewhere. It almost feels like three separate products. The team agrees we need **one clear, consistent narrative** throughout.
- **Specific wording change agreed:** Replace "turns Claude & GPT into technical assistant" → **"turns LLMs into technical assistant"** (avoids triggering Claude Code / Codex comparisons and is more accurate since we support multiple models).
- **"Safely executing work" — remove "safely."** It invites hard questions about guardrails we haven't fully built yet. Just say "executing work."
- **The distribution/moat slide is weak.** Team agrees being in Claude/ChatGPT ecosystems is an advantage vs. small competitors but not a real moat vs. big players. Needs rethinking — current wording ("hard to copy") is too defensive and the visualization is confusing.

### 3. Market Positioning Slide

- Eduard's view: we sit **between** OpenClaw (powerful but uncontrollable) and Cowork (safe but limited). We're not the most powerful and not the safest — we're in the middle, letting users set their own guardrails.
- The team debated whether to show competitors on the market slide or save it for the competition slide. Consensus: keep the market slide about **market evolution** (chatbots → coding assistants → AI employees), mention competitors briefly, and do the deep comparison on a separate competition slide.
